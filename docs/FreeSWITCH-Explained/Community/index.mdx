
# Community 

 

## **About[​](https://developer.signalwire.com/freeswitch/FreeSWITCH-Explained/Community/#about)**

The FreeSWITCH open-source project thrives on community and collaboration. Here you’ll find resources for where you can get involved with the project or get your questions answered. 

It doesn’t matter if you’re a seasoned developer or just getting started. There are many ways to get involved. You can contribute code and documentation or just participate in events and conversations.

The community welcomes all forms of support: ideas, code patches, donations of money and hardware, documentation efforts, or simply your participation in discussions.

## **Contribute Code[​](https://developer.signalwire.com/freeswitch/FreeSWITCH-Explained/Community/#contribute-code)**

FreeSWITCH is open to contributions from developers of all skill levels. Learn how you can get started and contribute code to the project [here.](https://developer.signalwire.com/freeswitch/FreeSWITCH-Explained/Community/Contributing-Code/#about)

## **Contribute Documentation[​](https://developer.signalwire.com/freeswitch/FreeSWITCH-Explained/Community/#contribute-documentation)**

Help make FreeSWITCH even more accessible by contributing to our documentation efforts. Find out more how to contribute to the documentation [here.](https://developer.signalwire.com/freeswitch/FreeSWITCH-Explained/Community/Contributing-Documentation/#about) 

## **Discord**

Our main community hub is now on Discord. This is where developers, the original team, and users gather to collaborate, share knowledge, and support each other in real-time. You’ll find help and community support by joining the Discord [here.](http://discord.gg/F2WNYTNjuF)

## **Support Portal**

The Support Portal includes a public forum where you can post questions about FreeSWITCH. It’s a great resource for tapping into the broader community’s experience and expertise. Post your questions [here.](https://signalwire.zohodesk.com/portal/en/community)

## **Office Hours**

We host Office Hours twice a month. These are free, public live sessions where you can bring your technical questions directly to the experts and work through them together in real time. It’s an excellent opportunity to learn and connect. [Sign up here.](https://info.signalwire.com/freeswitch-office-hours-signup)

## **ClueCon Annual Convention[​](https://developer.signalwire.com/freeswitch/FreeSWITCH-Explained/Community/#cluecon-annual-convention)**

ClueCon is our developer conference held every year in Chicago. Started by the FreeSWITCH core developers in 2005, ClueCon brings together the global open-source telecom community to share insights and innovations. Whether you’re building with FreeSWITCH, SignalWire, or anything in the programmable communications space, ClueCon is the place to be. [Register here.](http://cluecon.com/register)
